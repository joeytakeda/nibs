This was a job upon which Sandy doted.
From his baby years, fire had been both his
joy and his bane, for despite many threats and
whippings, the burning down of a costly barn

hotly in the memory of even a boy who loved

forevermore to regard matches with respect
and an element of fear. P. D. had deliberately
burned the tips of his son’s fingers. Though
Sandy feared the fire, he still loved it. With
both care and craft, therefore, he poked the
fire, and pounded the huge pieces of coal till
they spluttered and burst into flames. The
heat grew intense.
