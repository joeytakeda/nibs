Chapter XXV

As a boy, Cheerio’s inability swiftly to ex
plain or defend himself, had resulted in many
unjust punishments. He was not stupid, but
became easily confused, and with the best of
intentions, he bungled into unfortunate situa
tions. His brother, Reggie, swift-witted and
glib of tongue, was far better equipped to de
fend and care for himself than the often be
wildered and stammering Cheerio. He had
changed very little, and his love had made
him now almost obtusely blind.
As he hurried eagerly across the verandah
to meet Hilda who was hastening in her direct
way for that “show down” which her peace of
mind demanded, Cheerio held out toward her
the intended gift.

Like a tidal wave, the girl’s anger over
