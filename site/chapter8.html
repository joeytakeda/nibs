Chapter VIII

SITTING in the sunlight on the wide steps
of the ranch house, chin cupped in her hands,
her glance far off across the mountain tops,
her thoughts wandering over the seas that
stretched between the Dominion of Canada
and the Mother-land, Hilda McPherson came
out of her deep reverie to find the object of
her thoughts standing before her. He had a
book in his hand and with the sunny, engag
ing almost boyish smile that was characteristic
of him he was tendering it to the girl on the
steps.

Occasionally, as on this Sunday, Sandy

great house that should have been his own.
